I successfully navigated this difficulty by meticulously mapping out every functional dependency and questioning whether each attribute truly required the entire primary key to be identified, which allowed me to see that some data was actually dependent on intermediate fields.

Question 3

What is your most successful approach to learning this content?

My most successful approach to mastering this content was adopting a strictly incremental methodology for the normalization process. Instead of attempting to visualize a final 3NF schema immediately, I focused on verifying each rule in a linear sequence, starting with atomicity in 1NF and then moving on to isolate partial dependencies in 2NF before finally hunting for transitive links. By isolating each step, I could manage the complexity of the PetsCare data without feeling overwhelmed by the number of attributes, which made it much easier to catch subtle errors that might have been ignored if I had rushed directly into the final relational model.
